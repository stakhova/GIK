We caught up with the virtuoso at his home in Leeds to ask a few questions.

GIK:

I’m sure you’re asked this all the time, but how on earth do you get all those sounds out of one instrument?

JG:

I hit it! Ha ha! Seriously, a lot of the techniques I use are very old, very traditional but fell out of favour for a long time. For example, the retuning thing, where I play a string and then bend it with the tuning peg, is an old banjo trick, invented by Earl Scruggs in the 1950s. I just have a lot of influences, so you might hear techniques from heavy metal, flamenco, new age acoustic and jazz in the same song!
